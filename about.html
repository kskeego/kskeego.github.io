About Woodward Pizza

Hi — I’m Kyle. I grew up in metro Detroit and started making pizza back in 2002 after working in a few neighborhood pizzerias around the tri‑county area. Through college and beyond I kept making pies on the side, tinkering with dough and sauce wherever life took me.

After earning an engineering degree I worked in the auto industry across Detroit, Canada, and St. Louis. When the Great Recession hit, I pivoted to the energy industry and lived in West Virginia, Tulsa, Pittsburgh, Denver, Houston, and eventually Oklahoma City. In every city I looked for the “square pizza” I grew up with — pan‑baked, rectangular, airy inside with those caramelized edges and warm sauce added after the bake — and almost never found it.

My wife Danielle and I met in Denver and put down roots in Edmond in 2014. During the oil and gas slowdown I headed back to the University of Oklahoma for my MBA. I almost opened a pizzeria then, but took the scholarship and finished the program. A few years later, after a long brunch conversation with our friend Keith about how much we missed square pizza, we decided to go all‑in. We sold our old home and I cashed in my retirement because we believed in this — and then I got to work.

For nearly two years I tested recipes out of our house on nights and weekends. I tried different flours, tomatoes, cheeses, proof times, and bake temperatures — running A/B tests and feeding neighbors to collect feedback. On some weekends we baked 50+ pizzas to get it right. Danielle and our daughter welcomed folks in the front yard; our newborn son mostly supervised from a stroller.

Through a friend from OU I met the Icehouse Project developer. The moment we saw the spot we knew it could be the community hub we imagined — family‑friendly, walkable, with other small businesses around us. We finished construction and opened in March 2022, and the neighborhood showed up in force — many of the same people who taste‑tested those early pies.

Today we’re still family‑owned and operated, living here in Edmond. If you stop by, you’ll usually see me and Danielle in the restaurant, with our kids learning the ropes when school’s out. We make square pizza the way we grew up eating it in Detroit: baked in seasoned steel pans with a light, airy interior, crispy caramelized edges, and warm red sauce added on top after the bake. Even though the pizza is rectangular, in Detroit we just call it a “square.”
