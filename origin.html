The Origin of Detroit‑Style “Square” Pizza

Detroit‑style pizza was born in neighborhood bars and bakeries, baked in sturdy steel pans and cut into squares. It’s rectangular in shape, but in Detroit we simply call it a “square.” The hallmarks are a light, airy interior, crispy caramelized edges from baking up against the pan, and warm red sauce spooned on top after the bake.

As the style grew up in the Motor City, families passed down their own techniques and pan traditions. After the 2008 financial crisis, many Detroiters left the auto industry and moved across the country. They carried their cravings with them — and asked for the pizza they remembered. That wave helped spread Detroit‑style pizza nationwide. Our owner Kyle is one of those Detroit natives who missed the squares he grew up eating and decided to make them here in Edmond.

Today you’ll find Detroit‑style pizza from the Great Lakes to the Great Plains. Ours stays true to the spirit of the original: pan‑baked, rectangular “square” pizza with a delicate crumb, caramelized edges, and warm sauce added after the bake.
