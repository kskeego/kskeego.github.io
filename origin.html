The Origin of Detroit‑Style “Square” Pizza

Detroit‑style pizza was born in post‑war Detroit, baked in sturdy steel pans that helped create a crisp bottom and caramelized cheese edges. Over time, Detroiters simply called it square pizza — even though the pans are rectangular — and the name stuck.

Spreading Beyond Detroit

After the 2008 financial crisis, many Detroiters left the auto industry and moved across the country. They couldn’t find the square pizza they grew up eating, and demand for the style spread. Our owner, Kyle, is one of those Detroit natives who carried the tradition with him — eventually bringing it here to Edmond and the OKC metro.

How We Do It

At Woodward Pizza we keep the spirit and technique: seasoned steel pans, cheese to the edge for a caramelized crust, and warm sauce on top after the bake. It’s the square pizza we remember — served with Oklahoma hospitality.
